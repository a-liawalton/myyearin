My Year 2018 In...
  • Movies

  • 01.

    Besides the fact that Zazie Beetz totally jacked my go-to hairstyle, I enjoyed her performance; the strategically-placed 9 to 5 song by Dolly Parton; and the silly cameos of other actors I've enjoyed in the past.

    Deadpool 2
  • 02.

    12 years in the making, the follow-up to the childhood movie was a smashing success. The villian was carefully crafted and stylish to boot. And side note: I loved the short film Bao that played before the movie.

    Incredibles 2
  • 03.

    Everything about this movie was beautiful - the cinematography, costume design, setting and, of course, the actors.

    Crazy Rich Asians
  • 04.

    Amazing performance by Miss Marvel herself and the boy that plays her son. Creepy but powerful.

    Room
  • 05.

    Teen romance flicks are a personal weakness, and Netflix has come through clutch with multiple options. This was one of my favorites from this year.

    To All the Boys I've Loved Before
  • 06.

    I didn't expect to see this film, much less genuinely enjoy it. Thankfully, my boyfriend dragged me to the theatre to experience the ride of my life.

    Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse
  • 07.

    Although I feel ashamed to say that I didn't watch any of the predecessor films, I enjoyed the storytelling - I'm a killer for a spy-esque film - and the costume design. Cate Blanchett probably wins costume design of the year.

    Ocean's 8
  • 08.

    I grew up watching Halloween and was grateful that I got to see this timeline-bending sequel at the Cinerama. More of a nostalgia-pleaser than anything else, Halloween still makes my list for the year.

    Halloween
  • 09.

    Not an Amy Schumer fan by any means, I am a sucker for a good makeover movie. This was a clever take, touting the message that the greatest makeover is the one on our sense of confidence. (Other issues exist with the movie, but this central theme spoke to me.)

    I Feel Pretty
  • 10.

    I'd wondered why Sanaa Lathan had gone bald and didn't think anything of it. Netflix gains even more of my respect by showing an experience shared by many Black women - the Big Chop and the self-love journey that follows.

    Nappily Ever After
